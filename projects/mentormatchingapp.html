Mentor Matching App

UX Design Sprint

Problem Space

Your school wants to strengthen the community by encouraging experienced students to connect with new students and help them adjust to campus life. Design an experience that allows mentors and mentees to discover each other.

Consider the needs of both mentors and mentees, including how how to connect mentors to mentees.

Research
Defining Users

Before starting with the user research, I determined the scope of the project by defining the users.

Designing Survey

In order to collect quantitative data about the needs of mentors and mentees, I designed a survey using Google Forms. The survey was sent to both first and second year Master's students at Georgia Tech. Insightful data from some of the questions in the survey is shown below.

Interviews

In order to understand more about their motivation and experiences of previous mentoring experiences, I conducted semi-structured interviews with students who identified as mentors as well as mentees.

A list of sample questions from the interview have been included below. Depending on whether the participant was a mentor or a mentee, the questions were framed accordingly

  • What was your motivation for becoming a mentor/ seeking a mentor?

  • What qualities do you look for in your mentee/ mentor?

  • What was the process for getting matched to a mentee/ mentor?

  • How and when did you first meet your mentee/ mentor?

  • In what ways did having a mentee/ mentor benefit you?

  • How do you think can this mentorship program, like the one you are involved in, be improved?

Analysis of Research Data

Analysing the data that I collected, I identified the needs, prefernces and the pain points of the mentors and the mentees

Mentees need assistance on academic topics such as figuring out courses

Mentors want to connect with mentees based on their interests and hobbies

Mentors and mentees want to know more about each other before being matched

Mentors do not like that mentees are assigned on first come first serve basis

Mentees found answering long questions about themselves to be challenging

Mentors with multiple mentees found it tiring to solve repititive doubts

Personas

Based on these insights, I designed personas to make sure my design meets the goals and solves the challenges faced by both user groups.
Ideation
Brainstorming

Adressing the needs of the user, I had to design a solution that provides two-way connection opportunities to mentors as well as mentees and for mentors to connect with mentees without first come first serve basis. While meeting this requirement, I also had to keep in consideration that mentees prefer to have only one mentor and mentors may have a few (2-3) mentees.

Keeping the users anonymous would help me achieve this goal.

Hence, using anonymity as a central theme in the solution, I began to sketch a few wireframes that would help me imagine the user flow.

Design

With the concept developed, I designed the flow of the app to visualize how mentors and mentees would use the app, both for connecting with each other and on a regular basis.

Wireframe Design

After deciding the stages the user goes through, I designed the wireframe for each stage of the app. The wireframes below are the final iteration and are designed from a mentor's perspective.

Feedback and Iteration

After designing the wireframe, I received feedback from three users (2 mentors, 1 mentee).

1. Initially, the design allowed the user to fill the anonymous form after the onboarding process. However, the user may not fill the form and would be left out of the discovery experience. As a result, it was necessary to include the filling of the form process in the onboarding process.

2. Though the users realised that having the feature to save an anonymous profile to review it later is important, clarification was needed. The "Save forLater" Button was thus replaced by a bookmark icon button.

3. Finally, I made changes to the profile section to make sure the disclaimer of anonymity does not make user feel ambigious about what information is visible and what isn't.

High-Fidelity Design

Finally, after I was satisfied the iterated wireframes, I began designing the high-fidelity version of the app.

While designing the visuals for the UI, I kept the design mostly white with a few elements that used primary and secondary colors, the main color being blue (#4285f4). The font I used in the design is Roboto.

Future Scope

Coming up with designing solution for this prompt was definitely fun!

Through this design challenge, I explored how mentors and mentees can be anonymously paired with each other. Allowing two-way connection instead the one-way approach commonly used in pairing was challenging because even with two-way connection, mentees still had to be paired with one mentor at the end. I tried to solve this challenge by limiting the requests a mentee can recieve and by imposing a limit on the number of mentees a mentor can have. However, further work is required to make the pairing process more effecient.

Furthermore, this design focused on providing solution for mentors and mentees to discover and connect with each other. Fututre work could like into aspects of how, after pairing up, the mentor-mentee relationship can be maintained and strengthened as time progresses.
